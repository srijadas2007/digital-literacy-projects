## Cybercrime Case Study – UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI and online payment fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India, where attackers trick individuals into transferring money through digital payment applications. These scams exploit a lack of awareness and trust in online transactions.

A typical incident begins when the victim receives a phone call, SMS, or WhatsApp message from someone posing as a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support representative. The attacker creates a sense of urgency, such as claiming that the victim’s bank account will be blocked or that a refund is pending. The victim is then instructed to click on a link, download an app, scan a QR code, or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app. In many cases, the attacker falsely claims that scanning a QR code or entering a PIN is required to receive money, which is misleading. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN or approves the request, money is instantly transferred to the attacker’s account.

This type of fraud mainly targets college students, elderly individuals, and people who are new to digital payments. The consequences include immediate financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal or banking information.

This case highlights the importance of awareness, as even small mistakes in digital transactions can lead to serious consequences.
